VibeLife Game Manual

The complete guide to exploring, building, battling, and socializing in VibeLife — a voxel-powered social MMORPG.

What is VibeLife?

VibeLife is a multiplayer virtual world where players explore regions, claim land, build with 3D objects and voxel blocks, battle enemies in RPG combat, socialize with friends, trade on a dynamic marketplace, and express themselves through emotes, photos, pets, and more. The game runs on a Godot 4.6 client connecting to a real-time server via WebSockets and REST APIs.

1. Getting Started

Installation

VibeLife runs as a native Godot 4.6 application. Download the client for your platform (Windows, macOS, or Linux), extract, and launch. The client connects to a VibeLife server — you can join a public server or host your own.

Creating an Account

When you launch VibeLife, the left sidebar presents three authentication modes:

Guest Mode

Jump in instantly without creating an account. Choose a display name (or default to "Guest Voyager") and start exploring. Guest sessions are temporary — progress may not persist between sessions.

Register

Create a permanent account with a unique display name (up to 32 characters) and a password (minimum 4 characters). Your account, inventory, parcels, and progress are saved permanently. You start with 1,000 currency.

Login

Sign into an existing account with your display name and password. Your session lasts 8 hours and auto-extends as you play. All your parcels, items, friends, and progress are restored.

Joining a Region

  1. Enter your backend server URL (or select a saved profile)
  2. Click Refresh Regions to load available regions
  3. Select a region from the dropdown
  4. Choose your auth mode and credentials
  5. Click Join World

The client will authenticate, load the region's scene, objects, enemies, and voxel chunks, then connect via WebSocket for real-time updates. The status pill in the top bar tracks progress: Disconnected → Loading → Ready → Connected.

2. The World

Regions

The VibeLife world is divided into regions — discrete, named spaces that players can explore. Each region has its own terrain, objects, parcels, enemies, and atmosphere. Regions track their population in real-time and support many concurrent players.

The playable area in each region spans from -28 to +28 on both X and Z axes, with a height (Y) range of 0 to 4 for avatar movement (voxel terrain extends up to 64 blocks high).

Parcels & Land Ownership

Each region is subdivided into rectangular parcels defined by coordinate boundaries. Parcels are the foundation of the building and ownership system.

Parcel Tiers

TierDescriptionBuilding Access
PublicCommunity land, open to allAnyone can build objects and edit voxels
ResidentialClaimable by any playerOwner and collaborators only
PremiumPremium claimable landOwner and collaborators only

Claiming Land

To claim an unclaimed residential or premium parcel, navigate to it and click Claim Parcel in the build panel. Once claimed, only you (and any collaborators you add) can build on that parcel. You can also Release parcels you no longer need.

Collaborators

Parcel owners can invite other players as collaborators, granting them full building and voxel editing rights on that parcel. This enables group building projects.

Navigation & Teleportation

Move your avatar with WASD at 7.5 units/second. The third-person camera follows your avatar and can be orbited with the right mouse button.

Teleport Points

Create named waypoints at any location to fast-travel later:

  • Create waypoint — saves your current position and rotation with a custom name
  • List waypoints — view all your saved teleport points
  • Teleport — instantly travel to any saved waypoint
  • Cross-region teleport — travel between different regions; your position in the previous region is saved

Sitting

Right-click on objects containing "bench" or "chair" in their name to sit on them. Your avatar snaps to the furniture position. Use the Stand command to get up. While sitting, movement controls are disabled.

Day/Night Cycle

VibeLife features a dynamic 10-minute (600-second) day/night cycle:

  • Midnight — Deep blue ambient light, minimal sun energy (0.05)
  • Dawn — Warm orange hues at the horizon, gradually brightening
  • Noon — Full white sunlight, maximum energy (2.0)
  • Dusk — Deep orange sunset, gradually dimming

The sky, fog color, ambient light, and sun position all shift smoothly throughout the cycle, creating an immersive atmosphere.

Build Mode

Click Enable Build Mode in the left build panel to enter building mode. This unlocks object placement, selection, and manipulation tools. A grid overlay appears on the ground to aid alignment.

Tip: You can only build on parcels where you have permission — public parcels, parcels you own, or parcels where you're a collaborator.

Object Grouping

Select multiple objects and group them by name for batch operations. Grouped objects can be duplicated together with a position offset, making it easy to replicate structures.

Blueprints

Save your creations as blueprints — reusable templates that store the full arrangement of objects (asset type, position, rotation, scale). Blueprints can be:

  • Saved from your current selection
  • Loaded from your blueprint library
  • Placed to instantly recreate the entire arrangement
  • Sold on the marketplace for other players to purchase

Build Permissions

The building permission system determines where you can place and edit objects:

Permission Rules

  • Public parcels — Everyone can build freely
  • Your parcel — Full control over your claimed land
  • Collaborator — Full build rights if the owner added you
  • Unclaimed parcel — Cannot build; claim it first
  • Other player's parcel — Denied (message shows owner name)

Object permissions also include per-object flags: Allow Copy, Allow Modify, and Allow Transfer.

4. Voxel Engine

Voxel Basics

VibeLife features a full voxel block engine for Minecraft-style building and terrain. Press V to toggle voxel mode. A wireframe cursor highlights the target block within a 10-block reach.

ActionInput
Place blockLMB click (places on adjacent empty space)
Break blockRMB click (removes target block)

Chunk System

The voxel world is divided into 16 × 64 × 16 block chunks (16,384 blocks each). Chunks load dynamically in a radius around your position (up to 5×5 chunks visible). The client maintains a cache of up to 64 chunks, evicting old ones as you move. Chunk data is compressed using RLE (Run-Length Encoding) for efficient network transmission.

Block Types

The game includes 13 default block types:

Terrain Generation

Chunks are procedurally generated using layered sine/cosine waves at scales of 0.05, 0.07, and 0.12 to create natural-looking heightmaps. The terrain height ranges from 10–16 blocks with proper layering:

  • Bottom layers — Stone
  • Middle layers — Dirt
  • Top layer — Grass
  • Below water level (y=4) — Filled with water

Custom Blocks

Players can create their own block types (IDs 128+) through the Voxel Shop:

  • Name — Custom block name
  • Color — Custom color for rendering
  • Transparency — Whether light passes through
  • Hardness — How long it takes to break
  • Price — Marketplace selling price

Custom blocks are listed on the marketplace for other players to purchase and use in their builds.

Voxel Blueprints

Save voxel structures as blueprints — stored as arrays of block positions and types with width, height, and depth. Voxel blueprints can be listed on the marketplace for sale and instantiated by buyers in their own parcels.

Voxel Permissions

Voxel editing follows slightly different rules than object building:

  • Wilderness (outside any parcel) — Anyone can place/break blocks
  • Public parcels — Anyone can edit voxels
  • Your parcel / collaborator — Full voxel control
  • Other player's parcel — Denied

Regeneration

Health and mana regenerate every second while alive:

  • HP Regen — 2% of Max HP per second
  • Mana Regen — 3% of Max Mana per second

Attacking

Click on an enemy to attack. Two attack styles are available:

⚔️ Melee Attack

Damage = Strength × 1.0 − Enemy Defense
Mana Cost: None
Range: 5 units
Best for: Sustained combat, no resource cost

✨ Magic Attack

Damage = Strength × 1.5 − Enemy Defense
Mana Cost: 20 per cast
Range: 5 units
Best for: Burst damage, 50% stronger than melee

Critical Hits: Every attack has a 10% chance to deal double damage. Critical hits appear as large gold-colored floating numbers.

Minimum damage is always 1, even if the enemy's defense exceeds your strength.

Enemies

Each region spawns 3–5 enemies at its edges. Enemies have AI with multiple behavior states:

VariantHPSTRDEFDifficulty
🟢 Slime5052Easy
🦴 Skeleton80105Medium
🪨 Golem2001515Hard (tanky)
👻 Shadow100203Hard (glass cannon)
🐉 Drake5002520Boss

Enemy stats scale with their level: HP increases by 25% per level, Strength and Defense by 20% per level. Enemies can spawn at levels 1–5.

Enemy AI Behavior

  1. Idle — Standing still, scanning for players within 8 units
  2. Patrol — Wandering randomly within 5 units of spawn (20% chance per tick)
  3. Aggro — Player detected! Locks on to nearest target
  4. Chase — Moves toward player at 2 units/second
  5. Attack — Within 3 units, attacks every 1.5 seconds
  6. Dead — Defeated; respawns after 30 seconds at original spawn point

Enemies give up the chase if the player moves more than 15 units from the enemy's spawn point.

Death & Respawn

When your HP reaches zero:

  1. A red "You Died" overlay fades in for 3 seconds
  2. 5% of your currency is deducted as a death penalty
  3. Your HP and Mana are fully restored
  4. You respawn at the center of the nearest parcel in the region
  5. Your death counter increases
Warning: The death penalty can be significant at high wealth levels. Bank your currency by investing in marketplace items or sending it to an alt if you're heading into dangerous territory.

Leveling & XP

Defeating enemies awards 20 × enemy level XP. When your accumulated XP reaches the threshold, you level up automatically:

  • All stats are recalculated to new level values
  • HP and Mana are fully restored
  • Excess XP carries over to the next level
  • A gold "Level Up!" notification appears on screen
  • Maximum combat level: 100

Combat HUD

The combat HUD displays your vital stats at all times:

  • HP Bar (red, top-center) — Current / Max health
  • Mana Bar (blue, below HP) — Current / Max mana
  • XP Bar (gold, bottom-center) — Progress to next level
  • Level Label (gold text) — "Lv. X"
  • Floating Damage Numbers — Red for normal hits, gold for criticals; float upward and fade
  • Loot Notifications — Cyan text showing drops, visible for 2.5 seconds

6. Social Features

Chat

VibeLife features a rich chat system with multiple channels:

Region Chat

Type a message and press Enter to broadcast to everyone in your region. Messages are limited to 180 characters. A chat bubble appears above your avatar for 5 seconds showing your message to nearby players.

Whisper (Private Messages)

Type /w <username> <message> to send a private message to another player in your region. Whispers appear in purple text and are only visible to sender and recipient.

Group Chat

Members of a group (guild) can chat in their group channel. Messages are visible to all group members regardless of which region they're in.

Chat Colors

  • White — Normal region chat
  • Purple — Whisper messages
  • Gold — System notifications
  • Gray — Timestamps

Chat history is buffered (last 50 messages per region) and sent to you when you join a region.

Friends

Build your social network with the friends system:

  • Add Friend — Send a friend request (appears as "pending")
  • Accept — Accept a pending request to become friends
  • Remove — Remove a friend from your list
  • Block — Block a player to prevent them from whispering or interacting with you
  • Unblock — Remove a block

Your friends list shows each friend's online status, current region, and custom status message.

Offline Messages

Send messages (up to 1,000 characters) to friends who are offline. They'll see your messages when they next log in. Messages can be marked as read.

Emote Combos

When two players perform the same compatible emote within 3 seconds and 3 units of each other, a special combo effect triggers:

  • High-Five → "combo:high-five" — celebratory burst
  • Dance → "combo:dance-sync" — synchronized dance with green particles
  • Bow → "combo:mutual-bow" — respectful exchange
  • Wave → "combo:wave-sync" — friendly greeting effect

Combos display a floating colored label and a burst of 8 colored particle spheres.

Voice Chat

VibeLife supports spatial voice chat within regions:

  • Join/Leave — Connect to your region's voice channel (max 32 participants)
  • Mute — Stop transmitting your voice
  • Deafen — Mute all incoming audio (auto-mutes your mic too)
  • Push-to-Talk — Hold V to transmit (optional mode)
  • Volume — Adjust from 0.0 to 1.0

Spatial Audio

Voice volume scales based on distance using the formula: Volume = 1 − (distance / 30)². Players beyond 30 units are silent. This creates natural proximity-based conversations.

Presence & Status

Set your online status to communicate availability:

  • Online — Actively playing (default)
  • Busy — In a focused activity
  • Away — Auto-set after 10 minutes of inactivity
  • Invisible — Appear offline to others
  • Offline — Logged out

You can also set a custom status message (e.g., "Building my castle!" or "AFK for dinner").

7. Economy

Currency

VibeLife has an in-game currency system. New accounts start with 1,000 currency.

Earning Currency

  • Combat loot — Defeat enemies for guaranteed currency drops
  • Marketplace sales — Sell items to other players
  • Achievements & challenges — XP and currency rewards
  • Quest rewards — NPC quest completion
  • Gifts — Receive from other players

Spending Currency

  • Marketplace purchases — Buy items, blueprints, custom blocks
  • Gifts — Send currency to friends
  • Guild treasury — Deposit into your guild's bank
  • Death penalty — Lose 5% on death in combat

All transactions are logged with type, amount, and timestamp for full audit trail.

Marketplace

The marketplace is VibeLife's player-driven economy hub:

  • List items — Set a fixed price or create an auction
  • Browse — Filter by item kind, price range, and sort order
  • Buy — Purchase fixed-price items instantly
  • Price History — View the last 50 sales for any item to track market trends
  • Cancel — Remove your active listings

Listable Item Types

Outfit, Accessory, Tool, Pet, Decoration, Consumable, Voxel Blueprint, Custom Block

Trading

Direct player-to-player trading with the trade offer system:

  1. Create a trade offer specifying items and/or currency you're offering
  2. Specify what items and/or currency you want in return
  3. The recipient can accept, decline, or counter
  4. On acceptance, items and currency are exchanged atomically

Auctions

List items for competitive bidding:

  • Set a minimum bid amount
  • Auctions run for a set duration (default: 24 hours)
  • Bid currency is held (unavailable for other purchases) until you're outbid
  • When outbid, your held currency is automatically released
  • Highest bidder wins when the auction expires
