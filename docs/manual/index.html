PacketCraft Game Manual

The complete guide to everything you can do on a PacketCraft server.

Getting Started

PacketCraft transforms a standard Minecraft server into a social MMORPG platform with economy, land ownership, achievements, pets, events, and much more. When you join a PacketCraft server, your account is created automatically. No registration needed.

Tip: Install the PacketCraft Fabric mod for the best experience. It adds keybind-activated GUI screens for economy, social, marketplace, achievements, and events — no chat commands needed.

Your progress is tracked from the moment you join: balance, friends, parcels, achievements, pet bonds, and event participation. Everything persists across sessions.

Economy & Vibes

The PacketCraft economy is powered by Vibes, the universal currency. You start with a small balance and earn more by playing.

Earning Vibes

  • Complete achievements (each awards XP and sometimes Vibes)
  • Sell items on the marketplace
  • Win event prizes
  • Complete daily and weekly challenges
  • Receive payments from other players

Spending Vibes

  • Buy items from the marketplace
  • Bid on auctions
  • Pay other players with /pay <player> <amount>
  • Purchase blueprints from other builders
  • Guild treasury deposits
Note: Vibes are shared across all PacketCraft servers. Your balance travels with you.

Parcel Tiers

Parcels come in three tiers:

Public

Open to all players. Anyone can build here. Great for community projects and newcomers.

Standard

Claimable by any player. Once claimed, only the owner and invited collaborators can build.

Premium

Larger plots in prime locations. Same ownership rules as standard but with more space.

Claiming and Managing

  • Walk to an unclaimed parcel and use /parcel claim
  • Only you and your collaborators can place or remove blocks on your parcel
  • Transfer ownership or add collaborators through the API or admin tools
  • Release your parcel with /parcel release to make it available again

Homes

Set a parcel as your home for quick teleportation and visitor features.

  • Set home — Designate any parcel you own as your home
  • Teleport home — Instantly return to your home parcel
  • Privacy controls — Set your home as public, friends-only, or private
  • Ratings — Other players can rate and favorite your home
  • Featured homes — Top-rated homes appear on the showcase
  • Visitor tracking — See who has visited your home

Social

PacketCraft has a full social system built in. Connect with other players, form groups, and build communities.

Friends

  • Send friend requests with /friends add <player>
  • View your friends list and their online status
  • Send and receive offline messages
  • Block players you don't want to interact with
  • View player profiles with bio and avatar info

Groups & Guilds

Groups are player-created organizations with shared resources and hierarchy.

  • Create a group with a name and description
  • Invite members and assign roles (member, officer, leader)
  • Shared treasury — deposit and withdraw Vibes as a group
  • Guild parcels — assign parcels to the guild for shared building
  • Emblems and banners — customize your guild's visual identity
  • Alliances — form alliances with other guilds, accept or break them

Marketplace

The player-driven marketplace supports three types of transactions:

Fixed-Price Listings

List items at a set price. Buyers purchase instantly. Browse listings with /market or press M.

Auctions

List items for auction with a starting price. Other players place bids. Highest bidder wins when the auction ends.

Direct Trading

Propose a trade to another player with specific items and Vibes. The other player can accept or decline.

Storefronts

Set up a persistent storefront to sell your goods. Other players can browse your inventory, rate your store, and place commissions for custom work.

  • Price history tracking per item
  • Trending items feed
  • Commission system for custom orders

Levels & Titles

As you earn XP from achievements and challenges, you level up. Each level requires progressively more XP (level N requires 100 * N XP beyond the previous level).

Challenges

Challenges are rotating objectives that award bonus XP.

Daily Challenges (3 per day)

Randomly selected each day from these templates:

  • Send 5 chat messages (30 XP)
  • Place 3 objects (40 XP)
  • Visit 2 regions (35 XP)
  • Walk 200 distance units (25 XP)
  • Send 10 chat messages (50 XP)
  • Place 5 objects (60 XP)
  • Make a new friend (45 XP)
  • Teleport to another region (20 XP)

Weekly Challenges (2 per week)

Larger objectives with bigger rewards:

  • Send 50 chat messages this week (150 XP)
  • Place 20 objects this week (200 XP)
  • Visit 4 regions this week (175 XP)
  • Make 3 friends this week (200 XP)
  • Walk 500 distance units this week (125 XP)

Tricks

Pets learn tricks as you interact with them. There are 8 tricks:

  • Sit — Your pet sits in place
  • Roll Over — A full roll animation
  • Dance — Pet dances along with you
  • Jump — A happy hop
  • Spin — Quick spin in place
  • Wave — Pet waves at nearby players
  • Play Dead — Dramatic collapse
  • Fetch — Pet runs out and comes back

Interactions

  • Feed — Increases happiness and energy
  • Play — Builds your bond level. Higher bonds mean more tricks learned.
  • Pet — Quick interaction that boosts happiness
  • Summon/Dismiss — Call your active pet to your side or send them away
Tip: Playing with your pet has a chance to teach them a new trick. Pets also learn a new trick each time they level up.

Event Features

  • Events can be one-time or recurring (daily, weekly, monthly)
  • RSVP to events to get reminders
  • Events have optional attendee limits and prize pools
  • Event leaderboards track participation
  • View events with K (Fabric mod) or through the API
